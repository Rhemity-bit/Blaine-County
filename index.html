Standard Operating Procedures (SOP)

This Standard Operating Procedures manual outlines the policies, responsibilities, and conduct expected of all personnel within the Blaine County Sheriff's Office (BCSO). These procedures are based on federal and state law, constitutional standards, and time-tested rural law enforcement values. All deputies, cadets, and support staff are required to uphold these standards with integrity, accountability, and pride.

The BCSO exists to protect life and property, enforce the law fairly, and preserve the peace in Blaine County. We serve a hardworking, law-abiding community, and we expect our deputies to reflect the same grit, honesty, and independence that built this county.

Blaine County Sheriff's Office | Miranda Rights

State of San Andreas - Blaine County

Miranda Rights are constitutional protections that must be read to individuals in police custody before interrogation. These rights, derived from the Fifth and Sixth Amendments to the U.S. Constitution, ensure that individuals are aware of their legal protections during custodial questioning.

Failure to properly administer Miranda Rights can result in evidence being excluded from court proceedings. All Blaine County Sheriff's Office personnel must be thoroughly familiar with the proper administration of these rights.

ðŸ”¹ Full Version
FULL VERSION

"Alright, listen up. You have the right to remain silent â€” anything you say can and will be used against you in a court of law. You have the right to speak with an attorney and to have that attorney present during questioning. If you cannot afford an attorney, one will be provided for you by the state. Do you understand the rights I've just read to you? With these rights in mind, do you wish to speak to me now?"

ðŸ”¹ Quick Version
QUICK VERSION

"You have the right to remain silent. Anything you say can be used against you in court. You have the right to an attorney. If you cannot afford one, one will be provided. Do you understand these rights?"

ðŸ”¹ When to Administer
WHEN TO ADMINISTER

Miranda Rights must be administered when:

  • The individual is in custody (not free to leave)
  • The individual is being interrogated or questioned
  • The questioning is likely to elicit an incriminating response

Failure to provide Miranda warnings when required may result in statements being inadmissible in court.

ðŸ”¹ Proper Administration
PROPER ADMINISTRATION

To properly administer Miranda Rights:

  1. Ensure the individual is in custody
  2. Clearly state each right using the approved wording
  3. Confirm understanding by asking "Do you understand these rights?"
  4. Obtain voluntary waiver before proceeding with questioning
  5. Document the administration in your report

If the individual invokes their right to remain silent or requests an attorney, questioning must cease immediately.

Blaine County Sheriff's Office | PIT Maneuver Training

Precision Immobilization Technique

The Precision Immobilization Technique (PIT) is a high-risk vehicle intervention tactic used to safely terminate high-speed pursuits by forcing a fleeing vehicle to lose control and come to a stop. This maneuver requires extensive training, proper judgment, and strict adherence to department policy.

Only officers who have completed the Advanced Pursuit Training course and received certification from a qualified instructor are authorized to perform PIT maneuvers. All PIT maneuvers require prior approval from a Sergeant or higher-ranking officer.

STEP 1
POSITIONING

Position your patrol vehicle parallel to the rear quarter of the fleeing vehicle, maintaining a distance of 2-3 feet. Your front bumper should be aligned with the suspect vehicle's rear door.

Key Points:

  • Maintain equal or greater speed to the fleeing vehicle
  • Ensure you have a clear escape route
  • Verify no pedestrians or civilian vehicles in immediate vicinity
  • Communicate your intention to supervisor
STEP 2
CONTACT

Once properly positioned, gently turn your steering wheel toward the fleeing vehicle to make contact with its rear quarter panel.

Proper Contact Points:

  • Contact should be made with your front bumper to their rear quarter panel
  • Apply steady, controlled pressure
  • Do not jerk the wheel or oversteer
  • Maintain your lane position

Warning: Excessive force can cause loss of control of both vehicles.

STEP 3
TURN

As contact is maintained, smoothly turn your steering wheel in the direction of the fleeing vehicle.

Technique:

  • Transfer momentum to induce oversteer in the suspect vehicle
  • Apply gentle acceleration to maintain contact pressure
  • The goal is to cause the suspect vehicle's rear tires to lose traction
  • Do not attempt to "push" the vehicle for extended periods

The maneuver should be completed within 1-2 seconds of contact.

STEP 4
RELEASE & SECURE

Once the suspect vehicle begins to turn, immediately release contact and counter-steer to maintain control of your patrol vehicle.

Post-Maneuver Actions:

  • Brake smoothly and come to a controlled stop
  • Do not continue to push the suspect vehicle
  • Position your vehicle to block the suspect's path if necessary
  • Call for backup and medical assistance if needed
  • Secure the scene and approach with caution

Remember: Your safety and the safety of the public are paramount.

PIT MANEUVER IN ACTION
PIT MANEUVER SAFETY PROTOCOLS
  • Authorization Required: PIT maneuvers require prior approval from a Sergeant or higher-ranking officer.
  • Eligible Suspects: Only for fleeing vehicles after committing a violent felony or DUI with injury risk.
  • Environmental Conditions: Must be dry pavement, good visibility, and no adverse weather.
  • Speed Limit: Recommended maximum speed of 35 mph (higher speeds significantly increase risk).
  • Vehicle Type: Only standard passenger vehicles (not motorcycles, large trucks, or hazardous materials carriers).
  • Location Restrictions: Prohibited in residential areas, near schools, or in crowded urban centers.
  • Child Safety: Prohibited when children are visible in the suspect vehicle.
  • Medical Considerations: Consider potential medical emergencies that may explain erratic driving.
OFFICIAL TRAINING VIDEO
PIT MANEUVER CERTIFICATION
Training Requirements

All officers must complete the following to be certified for PIT maneuvers:

  • 8-hour classroom instruction on policy and legal considerations
  • 16-hour practical training on the driving course
  • Successful completion of 10 supervised PIT maneuvers
  • Written examination with 90% passing score
  • Annual recertification with 4 hours of refresher training

Certification is valid for one year from date of completion. Officers must document all PIT maneuvers in the after-action report.
